WHICH TOOTHPASTE IS BEST?

To understand which toothpaste is best, it’s important to know how toothpaste works.


Dental pellicle or biofilm is a transparent slimy layer that forms on the enamel surface by selective binding of proteins present in saliva. It forms in seconds after a tooth is cleaned or after chewing. This young, fresh biofilm is tooth-friendly at this stage.


However, bacteria soon start to adhere to the pellicle. Bacteria form a sticky mass from food deposits also known as plaque. If it is not removed, the new biofilm keeps on accumulating results in gum inflammation. Soon a friend becomes an enemy.


What does toothpaste do?


The fastest way to get rid of stickiness of oil from hands is to wash your hands with soil/fine sand. That's what toothpaste does, removing biofilm from tooth by abrasive action as an adjunct with toothbrush. Those particles can be anything Silica (sand), charcoal, salt, some ground herb etc. The task is mechanical, not chemical, so it is not important what it contains chemically.


So what’s the criterion?


The discretion we expect from toothpaste is to remove the sticky layer of plaque, without abrading Enamel. For that the particle size needs to be within a limit- below RDA 250.


Types of toothpaste


Children toothpaste: They are with low fluoride content than adults.
Sensitivity tooth paste: They are medicated tooth paste, should be used only when there is sensitivity to hot, cold, sour and sweets in multiple teeth. Prolonged usage of sensitivity toothpastes should be avoided. If single tooth is having sensitivity that might be because of infection so one should consult dentist.
Tooth whitening toothpaste: They contain cleansers and abrasives that help remove or lessen the appearance of stains.
Herbal toothpaste Dant Kanti or any other herbal toothpaste: is produced according to ayurvedic procedures. They are very abrasive and do harm in longer use.


Facts about toothpaste


Tooth paste contains nicotine which can make you addictive to them e.g Vicco, Senolin.


Toothpaste contains alcohol causes dryness of mouth might cause harm than good e.g Pepsodent 2in1, Colgate strong teeth.


Toothpaste without fluoride doesn’t protect you against cavities e.g Dabur red, Meswak.


Toothpaste containing both alcohol and tobacco should not be used e.g Colgate herbal, Himalaya complete care.


READ INGREDIENTS OF TOOTHPASTE BEFORE BUYING.


Take home message


It is the brushing technique which matters the most not the toothpaste.
